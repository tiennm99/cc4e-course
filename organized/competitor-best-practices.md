# Loyalty Program Best Practices — Web Research

## The Programs That Win Do Three Things Basecamp Rewards Doesn't

### 1. They Feel Personal
- Starbucks knows your order. Dutch Bros knows your name.
- The best programs use data to make customers feel *seen*, not like a transaction.
- Our own customers said: "I've been coming here 2 years but the app treats me like a stranger."
- Best practice: use purchase history to personalize rewards and offers per customer.

### 2. They Create Identity and Community
- Winning programs make customers feel like they belong to something — not just earn points.
- This is Basecamp Coffee's entire brand. We just haven't brought it into the rewards program.
- "Think of it as your secret handshake with your regulars."

### 3. They Make Earning Feel Achievable and Fun
- Gamification, progress nudges, small wins keep people engaged between visits.
- Effective messaging: "Only two stamps until a free coffee!" — progress visibility matters.
- Our customers said points don't motivate until they're close to a reward. Fixable.

## Key Stats

- 74% of restaurant leaders have a loyalty program; 80% find them effective
- Loyalty programs increase repeat visits (82%), order size (83%), and ROI (78%)
- 75% of customers are more likely to return to a business with a loyalty reward
- Retaining existing customers is 5–7x cheaper than acquiring new ones
- 42% of cafes identified using data to understand customers as critical to staying competitive

## What the Best Programs Look Like

| Practice | What It Means | Basecamp Today |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Simple structure | Easy to understand, easy to earn | Okay, but tiers confuse people |
| Personalization | Rewards based on preferences/history | None |
| Mobile-first | Points tracking always in pocket | App exists, but ignored |
| Gamification | Progress visible, milestones celebrated | None |
| Human connection | Staff know names, remember orders | Strong in-store, not in app |
| Multi-channel comms | Email, push, SMS with relevant content | Low open rates, generic content |

## The Gap

Our coffee creates community. Our rewards program doesn't.

Every data point — customer feedback, flyer review, engagement metrics — points to the same thing: the program is transactional. The brand is relational. Close that gap and we have something.

---

## The Opportunity

A coffee personality quiz (the idea Marcus kept coming back to) aligns perfectly with best practices:
- Makes customers feel known from day one
- Creates identity ("I'm a bold espresso person")
- Enables personalization of rewards and recommendations
- Feels like Basecamp — warm, playful, community-focused
