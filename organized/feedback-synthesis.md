# Customer Feedback Synthesis — January through April

## The Headline

**"Fine but forgettable."**

This exact phrase appeared in 3 separate months of feedback from different customers. It's not a coincidence. It's the diagnosis.

---

## Theme 1: Nobody Remembers the Program Exists

- "Honestly forgot I was a member until you emailed me this survey"
- "I have the app somewhere on my phone"
- "I always forget to scan my app"
- Customers are members on paper. Not in practice.

## Theme 2: The Tiers Mean Nothing

- "What's an Explorer? Am I supposed to care?"
- "I've been coming here a year and I'm still a Trailblazer. What does that even mean?"
- "Is there any reason to hit the higher tiers?"
- Baristas can't even explain the tiers when asked. If your own staff can't sell it, customers won't care.

## Theme 3: No Personality — Feels Generic

- "Generic. Could be any coffee chain."
- "This program has no personality. It's just points."
- "The program doesn't feel like Basecamp. It feels like corporate stuff."
- **The killer quote from April:** *"Your coffee has personality. Your rewards program doesn't. Fix that and you'd have something."*

People LOVE Basecamp Coffee. NPS of 67 overall vs. 12 for the rewards program. The brand is strong — the program just doesn't feel like it belongs to the brand.

## Theme 4: Customers Want to Feel Known

- "I don't feel like a valued customer, I feel like a transaction"
- "I've been coming here 2 years but the app treats me like a stranger"
- "People light up when we ask about their coffee preferences but the app doesn't capture any of that"
- "What if you helped people find their signature drink?"
- Dutch Bros gets mentioned positively for remembering names. We get mentioned for having good coffee — but the program doesn't extend that warmth.

## Theme 5: Promotions Get Short-Term Bumps, Nothing Sticks

- Valentine's Day double points: 23% spike, back to normal the next day
- Every promotion drives a temporary lift with zero lasting behavioral change
- The problem isn't awareness — it's that there's nothing compelling to come back for

---

## Satisfaction Trend (4 months)

| Month | Very Satisfied | Neutral | Dissatisfied+ |
|-------|---------------|---------|---------------|
| January | 12% | 41% | 13% |
| February | 10% | 44% | 15% |
| March | 8% | 47% | 16% |
| April | 7% | 48% | 18% |

Slowly getting worse every month.

---

## The Real Problem

People love Basecamp Coffee. They don't love (or even remember) Basecamp Rewards.

The program is transactional. Basecamp's brand is relational.

That's the gap we need to close.
